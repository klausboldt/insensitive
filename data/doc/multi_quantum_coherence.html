Multi-quantum coherence

Multi-quantum coherences are coherent superpositions of two or more spins in a system, linked by a defined phase relationship. For example, the multi-quantum coherence 2I1xI2x carries the information that if the magnetisation of spin 1 (red) points in x direction, the same holds for spin 2 (blue). However, the spins can also both point in -x direction with the same probability. With no preference for either positive or negative direction, multi-quantum coherences do not result in any observable magnetisation and are not visible in the vector representation of a spin system.

The -2I1xI2x operator represents those spins in which the coherences of the two couples spins point in the opposite direction (spin 1 points in x if spin 2 points in -x and vice versa.) Similarly, in case of the operator 2I1xI2y the magnetic moments of spin 1 (red) and spin 2 (blue) have a phase shift of +90°, again with equal probability that both coherences have either positive or negative sign.

The first summand is a double-quantum coherence, which precesses with the sum of the Larmor frequencies of the participating spins, Ω1 + Ω2. The second summand is the zero-quantum coherence that precesses with the difference frequency, Ω1 - Ω2. As with single-quantum coherences, precession turns multi-quantum coherences from x to y to -x to -y. Unlike single-quantum coherences, the coupling propagator has no effect on multi-quantum coherences, since all spins are in a mixed, coherent state and the populations of the spins are unknown.

The matrix representation of this operator has no non-zero matrix elements for populations or observable coherences. In many 2D techniques, multi-quantum coherences are used for transferring information between spins, like for example the HMQC experiment.
