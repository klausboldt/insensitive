Multi-quantum coherence

Multi-quantum coherences are states of two or more spins in a system linked to have the same transverse magnetisation (e.g. 2I1xI2x).

This state of higher coherence does not result in any observable magnetisation and is not visible in the vector representation of a spin system. It is the basis for transferring information between spins in many 2D techniques like for example HMQC.

In the case of the operator 2I1xI2y the magnetic moments of spin 1 (red) and spin 2 (blue) have a phase shift of 90°, but still result in no net magnetisation.

The matrix representation of this operator has no non-zero matrix elements for populations or observable coherences. The multi-quantum coherences precess with the sum of the Larmor frequencies of the participating spins. The coupling propagator has no effect on multi-quantum coherences, since all spins are in a mixed, coherent state and the populations of the spins are unknown.
