NMR Tutorial

The nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) experiment makes use of the magnetic moment associated with the spin of atomic nuclei to gain information on the chemical structure of molecules and solids. Understanding the physics behind spin dynamics can be challenging. Insensitive is an application that helps with that by visualising the NMR experiment on the level of a spin ensemble. The spin system is represented as a semiclassical vector model, as a spin density matrix and as a sum of product operators. Additionally the energy level diagram of the spin system can be displayed.

Read the user's manual for instructions to use Insensitive. The spectrometer hardware is explained here.

Quantum mechanics

It is important to understand that spin is an inherently quantum mechanical property that has no equivalent in the macroscopic world. Every visualisation in three-dimensional space is bound to fail at some point and carries the danger of misunderstanding spin. Insensitive aim is therefore not only to illustrate textbook spin dynamics, but also to demonstrate the limits of different spin models. A god argument can be made that spin should not at all be visualised using the vector model. However, since it is taught and used in many textbooks and classes on NMR, Insensitive serves as a tool understand as much as unlearn spin as literal orbital momentum.

Pulse sequences

Insensitive can simulate a wide range of homo- and heteronuclear experiments in one and two dimensions. The tutorial includes a list of pulse sequences for many NMR experiments with instructions how to set them up and understand their actions on the spin ensemble.

Fourier Transformation

The pulsed NMR experiments make use on the Fourier transformation, which transforms the free induction decay recorded in the time domain into a spectrum in the frequency domain.

Further reading

This tutorial is by no means a comprehensive introduction to NMR spectroscopy. The program should be used in combination with a textbook. Spin Dynamics by Malcolm Levitt and Understanding NMR Spectroscopy by James Keeler are highly recommened as companions for using Insensitive. Spin by Ilya Kuprov gives a rigorous treatment of spin from basic symmetries. All literature references that were used for writing Insensitive are listed in the bibliography.
