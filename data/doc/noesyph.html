NOESY

The NOESY is a 2D technique to measure intramolecular distances employing the Nuclear Overhauser Effect (NOE). It is based on the exchange of magnetisation via dipole-dipole coupling during relaxation of the spins back to thermal equilibrium.

The pulse sequence consists of three 90°-pulses. The first and second pulse are separated by the variable time t1, followed by a constant mixing time τm, the third pulse and the acquisition of the FID.

Set up a two-spin system of I-spins with Ω1 = 0 Hz and Ω2 = 5.5 Hz. Set up no scalar coupling and set the distance between the two spins to 0.27 nm. Make sure that dipolar coupling is selected in the Settings. For better understanding at first only apply relaxation during the mixing time τm.

After the first pulse coherences between the two spin states are created, which start to precess in the transverse plane depending on their respective chemical shifts. The product operators at this time are

- I1y cos(Ω1t1) + I1x sin(Ω1t1)
- I2y cos(Ω2t1) + I2x sin(Ω2t1)

The second 90°-x-pulse turns y- into -z-magnetisation and -y- into z-magnetisation:

+ I1z cos(Ω1t1) + I1x sin(Ω1t1)
+ I2z cos(Ω2t1) + I2x sin(Ω2t1)

During the relaxation period that follows the second pulse the cross-relaxation between spin 1 and 2 causes magnetisation transfer following the Solomon equations.

Omitting the x-magnetisation terms which will be suppressed by phase cycling the spin system will relax with the transverse relaxation rate λ, the transition amplitude

adiag = cosh(Rcrossτm) exp(-Rautoτm)

which will cause the diagonal peaks, and the transition amplitude

across = sinh(Rcrossτm) exp(-Rautoτm)

which will cause the cross peaks, to the product operator term

+ I1z adiag(τm) cos(Ω1t1) exp(-λt1)
+ I1z across(τm) cos(Ω1t1) exp(-λt1)
+ I2z adiag(τm) cos(Ω2t1) exp(-λt1)
+ I2z across(τm) cos(Ω2t1) exp(-λt1)

The last pulse turns the longitudinal magnetisation back into the transverse plane for detection. The magnetisation which is transferred via across from spin 1 to spin 2 depends only on the transverse magnetisation of spin 1 up to the second pulse and only evolves with the Larmor frequency of spin 2 after the mixing. It can therefore give rise to a peak at Ω1 in the F1-domain and at Ω2 in the F2-domain after 2D-Fourier transformation.

Both diagonal and cross peaks in the NOESY spectrum appear with an absorption line shape. For molecules with short correlation times τc the cross peaks have the opposite sign of the diagonal peaks, while larger molecules with long correlation times have the same sign for all peaks. For medium sized molecules the peaks can disappear completely. In this case the ROESY experiment can be used to determine intramolecular distances. For J-coupled spins the NOESY spectrum shows COSY artefacts, which have dispersive line shapes.
