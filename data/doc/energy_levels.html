Energy level diagrams

In a magnetic field the possible energy eigenstates of a spin 1/2 split up into to energy levels, called Zeeman splitting. The levels are denominated α for the lower state and β for the higher state. In a system of more than one spin each energy level corresponds to one configuration of each spin in the system, e.g. αβα in a system of three spins.
In the very simple energy level description of NMR peaks in the spectrum are treated as transitions between states. The energy levels of a spin system reveal the relative positions of the eigenstates on an energy scale, the Zeeman splitting and transition energies. This model treats peaks in the spectrum as transitions between states. A transition between two levels where only one spin is changed corresponds to an observable, Single-quantum transition. The energy level diagrams do not account for coherences, superposition states and phenomena such as coherence transfer.

For a weakly coupled spin system of two spins the energy diagram can be constructed by first drawing the splitting of one spin into the levels α and β and then splitting these levels according to the second spin's resonance frequency. This yields four energy levels with four observable transitions, each two with the same energy difference.

The coupling interaction increases or decreases the energy values and creates the typical doublet that splits symmetrically around the spins' resonance energies.

If two spins are magnetically equivalent, the splitting leads to two degenerate eigenstates. In this case the states αβ and βα mix. The system is best described in the singlet-triplet basis, for which the Hamiltonian matrix takes diagonal form. The system comprises of three energy levels forming a triplet state

and one level forming a singlet state.

The transition between triplet and singlet states is spin forbidden, which leads to the disappearance of two transitions in the spectrum. The doublet of each coupling partner collapses to a single line.

In the case of strong coupling the mixing between the αβ and βα states is incomplete with the degree of mixing described by the angle ξ.

The energy levels can no longer be assigned to well defined states of each spin, since the two spins are indistinguishable. Intead, both antiparallel states of the Zeeman basis contribute to each energy level.

This results in two transitions being less probable in comparison to the other two. In the spectrum one observes the 'roofing' effect of strongly coupled doublets, where the peaks that are further apart in a pair of coupled spins appear weaker than the peaks that are closer in energy.

The spectral lines in a strongly coupled doublet are no longer symmetrically arranged around the resonance energy. The shift of the partially mixed energy levels is given by the strong coupling parameter.
