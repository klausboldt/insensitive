BIRD and TANGO pulses

The BIRD and TANGO blocks are sequences that can be found as part of larger pulse sequences. They are designed to distinguish between protons bound to 12C and 13C atoms.

The BIRD pulse

The BIRD sequence inverts protons attached to 12C atoms while leaving those bound to NMR-active 13C nuclei unaffected. This created a 180° phase shift between the different I spins.

The spin echo in the BIRD pulse refocusses the proton spins that are not bound to an NMR-active nucleus and does not have any impact on the spin state apart from relaxation. Any S spin will get inverted by the 180° pulse as well. The terms

- I1y cos(Ωt1) cos(πJ12t1)
+ 2I1xI2z cos(Ωt1) sin(πJ12t1)
+ I1x sin(Ωt1) cos(πJ12t1)
+ 2I1yI2z sin(Ωt1) sin(πJ12t1)

before the refocussing pulse get turned into

- I1y cos(Ωt1) cos(πJ12t1)
+ 2I1xI2z cos(Ωt1) sin(πJ12t1)
- I1x sin(Ωt1) cos(πJ12t1)
- 2I1yI2z sin(Ωt1) sin(πJ12t1)

leading to a 180° phase shift of any I spins that are coupled to an S spin in comparison to uncoupled I spins.

The TANGO pulse

Depending on the phase of the last 135° pulse the TANGO sequence selectively excites protons bound to 12C atoms (φ = 0°) or protons bound to 13C atoms (φ = 180°). The carbon spins are inverted.

The spin echo in this block does essentially the same as in the BIRD pulse. The difference is that due to the initial 45° pulse there is an angle of 90° between the coupled and uncoupled I spins in the y,z-plane after the second delay, as opposed to 180° for the BIRD pulse. (The phase shift in the x,y-plane is 180° for either block.) The final 135° pulse always turns one of the vectors back towards the z-axis while the other will end up as fully observable y-magnetisation.
