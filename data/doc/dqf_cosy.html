DQF-COSY

The Double Quantum Filtered COSY experiment uses a third 90° pulse after the COSY sequence to show those signals in the spectrum that have been converted to double quantum coherences after the second pulse.

This technique causes all diagonal and cross peaks to appear in pure absorption mode in the spectrum.

To select the coherence pathway for an N-type spectrum a 4 step phase cycle can be used that varies the phase of the first and third pulse φ1 = {0°, 90°, 180°, 270°} and φ2 = {0°, 90°, 180°, 270°} and the receiver phase φrec = {0°, 180°} while keeping φ3 constant at 0°.

Step φ1 φ2 φ3 φrec
1 0° 0° 0° 0°
2 90° 90° 0° 180°
3 180° 180° 0° 0°
4 270° 270° 0° 180°

The number of phase cycles is further increased to eight when using the States method to create pure phase spectra.
To select the coherence pathway by pulsed field gradients three gradient pulses have to be applied, which reduces the number of phase cycles to 2, which also need to be acquired in an amplitude modulated mode.

The first two PFGs select the +1 and +2 coherences after the first two pulses respectively, while the third PFG with three times the strength of the first two gradient pulses dephases all but the -1 coherences.
