Pulse sequences

1H-Spectrum

The simplest pulsed NMR experiment. (Bruker code: zg)

CMPG

The Carr-Purcell-Meiboom-Gill sequence allows measurement of the transverse spin-spin relaxation time T2. (Bruker code: cpmg and cpmg1d)

COSY

The COrrelated SpectroscopY that shows J-coupling as cross peaks was the first two-dimensional NMR experiment. (Bruker code: cosyqf)

APT

The APT (Attached Proton Test) experiment was designed to show the number of attached protons to carbon atoms while decoupling is used. (Bruker code: jmod and apt)

DEPT

The DEPT (Distortionless Enhancement by Polarisation Transfer) experiment was designed to improve the sensitivity of NMR experiments on low-abundant and low magnetogyric ratio nuclei. (Bruker code: dept)

DOSY

The Diffusion Ordered Spectroscopy allows to measure diffusion constants and hydrodynamic radii in an mixture of compounds. (Bruker code: stegp1s)

DQF-COSY

The Double Quantum Filtered COSY causes narrower spectral lines than the COSY experiment on the costs of signal strength. (Bruker code: cosydfqf)

E.COSY

The exclusive COSY or E.COSY is a difference spectrum of a double and a triple quantum filtered COSY spectrum, which helps to resolve small coupling constants. (Bruker code: ecos2nph and ecos3cph)

HETCOR

The 2D Heteronuclear Chemical-Shift Correlation (HETCOR) experiment is the heteronuclear analogue to the COSY experiment. Due to detection on the X-heteronuclei the HMQC and HSQC experiments are preferred in routine measurements. (Bruker code: hxcoqf)

HMBC

The 2D HMBC (heteronuclear multiple-bond correlation) experiment permits to obtain a 2D heteronuclear Chemical Shift correlation map between long-range coupled 1H and heteronuclei. (Bruker code: hmbclpndqf)

HMQC

The 2D HMQC (heteronuclear multiple-quantum correlation) permits to obtain a 2D heteronuclear chemical shift correlation map between directly-bound 1H and X-heteronuclei via multi-quantum coherences. (Bruker code: hmqcqf)

HSQC

The 2D HMQC (heteronuclear single-quantum correlation) permits to obtain a 2D heteronuclear chemical shift correlation map between directly-bound 1H and X-heteronuclei via single-quantum coherences. (Bruker code: hsqcph)

INADEQUATE

The INADEQUATE (Incredible Natural Abundance Double Quantum Transfer Experiment) experiment permits to obtain information derived from 13C-13C couplings and can be used to establish the identity of adjacent carbon atoms in a molecule. In addition, from INADEQUATE spectra, direct and long-range 13C-13C coupling constants can be measured. (Bruker code: inad1d)

INEPT

The INEPT (Insensitive Nuclei Enhancement by Polarisation Transfer) experiment was designed to improve the sensitivity of NMR experiments on low-abundant and low magnetogyric ratio nuclei. (Bruker code: ineptnd and ineptrd)

Inversion-Recovery

The inversion-recovery experiment allows to measure longitudinal or spin-lattice T1 relaxation times. (Bruker code: t1ir1d)

J-Resolved

The 2D J-Resolved spectroscopy is used to simplify a spectrum by separating chemical shift and coupling information into two dimensions. (Bruker code: jresqf)

NOESY

The 2D NOESY (Nuclear Overhauser SpectroscopY) experiment offers a simple way to obtain all NOE information in a molecule by a single experiment and without prior knowledge of the spectral assignment or molecular structure. (Bruker code: noesyph)

PENDANT

The PENDANT experiment (Polarisation ENhancement During Attached Nucleus Testing) is similar to the DEPT sequence, but also shows quarternary carbon atoms in the spectrum. (Bruker code: pendant)

Relayed COSY

The Relayed COSY experiment shows cross peaks for couplings over three and four bonds (in contrast to the simple COSY, which only shows coupling over three bonds). (Bruker code: cosyqfri)

ROESY

The Rotating-frame Overhauser SpectroscopY offers means to obtain NOE information in larger molecules with short T1-times. (Bruker code: roesyph)

SECSY

The Spin Echo Correlated SpectroscopY is comparable to the COSY experiment, but produces a skewed spectrum that uses half the amount of storage. (Bruker code: secsyqf)

TOCSY

The 2D TOCSY (TOtal Correlation SpectroscopY) experiment (also called HOHAHA (HOmonuclear HArtmann-HAhn) experiment) permits to assign a whole spin system regardless of the exact topology. (Bruker code: mlevph)

TROSY

The Transverse relaxation optimised spectroscopy allows for the measurement of large molecules with very short T2 times.

z-COSY

The z-COSY experiment uses a z-filter to dephase unwanted coherences and produce a COSY spectrum with clean signals and narrow diagonal peaks. (Bruker code: cosygpzfqf)
