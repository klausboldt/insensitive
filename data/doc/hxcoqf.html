HETCOR

The HETeronuclear CORrelation spectroscopy or H,X-COSY is the heteronuclear analogue to the COSY experiment. It shows 1J-couplings between protons and a hetero nucleus, usually 13C (Bruker code: hxcoqf). Due to detection on the X-nucleus the faster, proton-detected HMQC or HSQC experiments are preferred.

Set up a two-spin system with one I-spin with a chemical shift of Ω1 = 0.5 Hz and an S-spin with a chemical shift of Ω2 = 0.25 Hz. Set the coupling constant to J = 0.5 Hz.

The first pulse creates transverse magnetisation of the I-spins of which only the scalar coupling evolves due to the spin echo sandwich over the evolution time t1. This is followed by an evolution time that is optimised to match half the coupling constant J and creates pure antiphase magnetisation of the I-spin in y-direction. The product operator terms at this point are -2I1yS2z - 0.25 S2z. A simultaneous 90°-pulse on both I- and S-spins creates in-phase magnetisation of the S-spin that is modulated by the chemical shift Ω2 and transfers the antiphase magnetisation from the I-spin to the S-spin over an intermediate state of longitudinal two-spin order. The product operators should now be 0.25 S2y + 2I1zS2y. The antiphase term is modulated by the chemical shift Ω1 of Spin 1 and gives rise to cross peaks in the spectrum. The transferred magnetisation is enhanced by the INEPT transfer. The magnetisation is allowed to evolve into in-phase magnetisation over a time τ = 1/3 J and the FID is acquired. The experiment uses phase cycling to suppress the in-phase signals.
