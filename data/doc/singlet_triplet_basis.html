Singlet-Triplet basis

In the case of two magnetically equivalent spins with the same Larmor frequency the operator for the strong coupling can be diagonalised and solved by choosing a different operator basis called singlet-triplet basis. It consists of the three triplet states

and one singlet state

The states T0 and S0 are superpositions of the αβ and βα states of the Zeeman basis, the other triplet states are the same as the Zeeman states αα and ββ. The spin pair as a quantum mechanical unit in its triplet state behaves like a spin with I = 1 and mI of either +1, 0 or -1. The singlet state behaves like a spin with I = 0 and is not associated with a magnetic moment. If a spin 1/2 couples to these spins its peaks will appear as a triplet with the intensity distribution 1:2:1 in the spectrum where the centre line consists of contributions from the triplet and singlet state.

Similarly, three magnetically equivalent spins mix to form a quartet (I = 3/2) and two doublet states (I = 1/2) with an intensity distribution of 1:3:3:1. Four magnetically equivalent spins behave like the combination of one quintet (I = 2), three triplets (I = 1), and two singlets (I = 0) with an intensity distribution of 1:4:6:4:1. The peak intensities of more magnetically equivalent spins can be calculated from Pascal's triangle.

The first part of the pulse sequence populates the states αβ and βα which is equal to the product operators I1z - I2z. The spin system is then moved to a low magnetic field in which the spins become magnetically equivalent.

The exchange of magnetisation between the singlet and triplet states by dipole-dipole coupling are symmetry forbidden. Thus, dipolar relaxation of the singlet state is suppressed and the state is stable for much longer than T1 in the order of minutes.

The magnetisation can then be brought back to the high magnetic field and transformed into observable coherences.
