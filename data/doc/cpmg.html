The transverse relaxation can be divided into the entropic dephasing with the relaxation time T2 and a contribution of magnetic field inhomogenieties which enhance the dephasing and decrease the relaxation time to a smaller value T2*. It is this time constant that determines the dampening of the free induction decay. To measure T2 the Carr-Purcell-Meiboom-Gill sequence has been devised, which begins with a 90°x pulse that creates transverse magnetisation. These coherences start to precess and relax. In the middle of a variable time τ a 180°y-pulse is applied to the system. This spin echo sandwich refocusses the spins. Acquisition of the FID begins after τ at the maximum of the spin echo.

By increasing number of 180° pulses one generates a number of spin echoes, which have a decreasing maximum intensity.

Through the spin echo the system regains only the magnetisation lost to inhomogenieties in the magnetic field. The transverse relaxation time T2 appears therefore as the decay time of the spin echo intensities as a function of τ.
