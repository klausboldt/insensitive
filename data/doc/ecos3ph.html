E.COSY

The exclusive COSY or E.COSY experiment is designed to yield correlation spectra, which resolve even small coupling constants in the cross peaks. In this it is similar to the COSY-45 or z-COSY, but shows less distortion from dispersive diagonal peaks and quantitative suppression of unwanted signals. It is a difference spectrum of a double and a triple quantum filtered COSY spectrum (DQF-COSY and TQF-COSY).

The normal (DQF) and complementary (TQF) component are based on the same pulse sequence, but differ in their phase cycles. The DQF-COSY has 4 steps, assuming use of the States method for data aquisition.

Step φ1 φ2 φ3 φrec
1 0° 0° 0° 0°
2 90° 90° 0° 180°
3 180° 180° 0° 0°
4 270° 270° 0° 180°

The TQF-COSY consists of two more cycles to select coherence pathways via triple quantum coherences.

Step φ1 φ2 φ3 φrec
1 0° 0° 0° 0°
2 60° 60° 0° 180°
3 120° 120° 0° 0°
4 180° 180° 0° 180°
5 240° 240° 0° 0°
6 300° 300° 0° 180°

Three coupled spins are necessary for the E.COSY to work. The spectrum will then appear with simplified multiplet structure of the cross peaks, which only show half of the signals of the standard COSY spectrum. They can be used to measure exact J-coupling between the spins.

Due to the necessity of acquiring two sets of data the sensitivity of the experiment is decreased 2-fold compared to the DQF-COSY, and 4-fold compared to a normal COSY spectrum. The experiment can be generalised to more than three spins.
