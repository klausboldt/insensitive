Spin echo sandwich

One of the most frequently used pulsed sequences is the spin echo sandwich that consists of an evolution period τ with an 180°-pulse in the middle.

The spin echo sequence starts with transverse magnetisation -Iy that is allowed to freely evolve for half of the evolution period τ/2. The coherences are affected by chemical shift which leads to the product operators

- Iy cos(Ωτ/2) + Ix sin(Ωt/2)

and coupling which creates antiphase magnetisation

- Iy cos(Ωτ/2) cos(πJτ/2)
+ 2IxSz cos(Ωτ/2) sin(πJτ/2)
+ Ix sin(Ωτ/2) cos(πJτ/2)
+ 2IySz sin(Ωτ/2) sin(πJτ/2)

The 180°x-pulse inverts all magnetisation in y- and z-direction. Since both spins are affected in the term 2IySz the product operator does not change sign.

+ Iy cos(Ωτ/2) cos(πJτ/2)
- 2IxSz cos(Ωτ/2) sin(πJτ/2)
+ Ix sin(Ωτ/2) cos(πJτ/2)
+ 2IySz sin(Ωτ/2) sin(πJτ/2)

In the second half of the evolution time the chemical shift evolves in the opposite direction, while the coupling continues unaffected except for being flipped around 180°. By using the relations

the product operators can be calculated as

+ Iy cos(πJτ) - 2IxSz sin(πJτ)

The chemical shift is refocussed while the coupling can evolve unhindered. In one's mind one can substitute the spin echo sequence by evolution of the coupling terms only followed by an 180°-pulse.

In the heteronuclear case one has three different options. If the 180°-pulse affects both the I-spins and the S-spins the result is the same as with the homonuclear case: The chemical shift is refocussed while the coupling is not.

If the pulse is applied to only the I-spins the chemical shift of the I-spins as well as the coupling gets refocussed while the S-spins evolve normally under the chemical shift.

If the pulse is applied to the S-spins only it refocusses the coupling and the chemical shift of the S-spins.

Spin echo sandwiches can be part of larger blocks such as the BIRD or TANGO pulses.
