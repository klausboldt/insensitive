INADEQUATE

The signal of 13C is weak due to its low natural abundance of 1.1% of all carbon nuclei. The probability to observe 13C-13C J-coupling is even smaller, and satellite signals of adjacent isotopes disappear below the stronger singlets in a conventional 13C spectrum. The Incredible Natural Abundance Double QUAntum Technique is a pulse sequence to selectively enhance the signal of natural pairs of 13C nuclei while suppressing the signals from isolated 13C spins.

The pulse sequence consists of four pulses on the S-spins, which allow the coupled spins to gain a phase shift compared to the uncoupled spins and be selected by phase cycling. The first 90°x-pulse creates transverse magnetisation, followed by a spin echo sandwich in which only the scalar coupling evolves. The period is set to be τ = (2J)-1 to achieve antiphase magnetisation at the end of the spin echo. At this point the product operators are S1y and S2y for two uncoupled spins or -2S1xS2z and -2S1zS2x for a pair of coupled spins.

The first 90°x-pulse of a pair of closely followed pulses transforms the antiphase terms into double quantum coherences 2S1xS2y and 2S1yS2x. The phase of the second 90°φ-pulse is varied by phase cycling between the positions φ4 = {90°, 180°, 270°, 0°}, while the receiver phase is set to φrec = {0°, 270°, 180°, 90°}. This creates antiphase terms again and transfers the magnetisation from one spin to the other. The product operators are now -2S1zS2y and -2S1yS2z. Uncoupled spins will be transformed into in-phase magnetisation with a 90° phase shift compared to coupled spins and are removed by the phase cycling. The signals in the spectrum will therefore appear as characteristic antiphase peaks.

The sample is irradiated with a proton decoupling sequence throughout the pulse sequence to remove couplings between 1H and 13C nuclei. A low-level irradiation on the proton channel can be applied to enhance the signal over NOE.

The INADEQUATE sequence can be easily extended into a two-dimensional experiment by inserting a variable evolution time t1 between the last two pulses. In this period the double quantum coherences evolve and pairs of coupling spins appear in different rows of the spectrum after Fourier transformation, depending on the sum of their chemical shifts Ω1 + Ω2.
