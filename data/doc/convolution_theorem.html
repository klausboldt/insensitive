Convolution theorem

Convolution is the operation of one function g continuously shifting over a second function f and taking the integral of the overlap of f and g as a function of the shifting time τ.

The convolution theorem states that the Fourier transformation of the convolution of two functions f and g is equal to the Fourier transformation of f multiplied by the Fourier transformation of g.

This can be used to predict the line shape of NMR spectra.
