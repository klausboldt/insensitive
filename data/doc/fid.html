Free induction decay

If a spin system with observable, transverse magnetisation is left alone the magnetisation vectors will precess around the z-axis, coupling between spins will create and destroy antiphase magnetisation and relaxation will return the system to thermal equilibrium. If this signal is detected by measuring the induction in a receiver coil, one acquires a dampened sine and cosine signal called the free induction decay or FID. The time constant of the dampening is the transversal relaxation time T2*.
The NMR spectrum is the Fourier transform of the FID.

Quadrature detection

In order to tell the difference between clockwise and counterclockwise precession relative to the reference frequency in the rotating frame, it is necessary to detect a complex signal with two orthogonal detectors in the x,y-plane. In the actual spectrometer hardware this is implemented by splitting the receiver signal in two and phase shifting one of them by 90°. This method is called quadrature detection. The phase shift works fine for directly detected FIDs, but has to be introduced by other methods for the indirect time variable in 2D spectra.
