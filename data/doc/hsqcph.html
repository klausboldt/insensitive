HSQC

The Heteronuclear Single-Quantum Correlation experiment is a two-dimensional method to record single-bond correlation spectra between protons and hetero spins. It is very similar to the HMQC experiment, but is better suited for large molecules due to different relaxation behaviour. For the HSQC spectrum the magnetisation of the protons is being detected, which results in higher signal strength compared to heteronuclear COSY.

Set up a two-spin system with one I-spin with a chemical shift of Ω1 = -1.8 Hz and an S-spin with a chemical shift of Ω2 = 2.5 Hz. Set the coupling constant to J = 0.25 Hz.

The HSQC experiment starts with a spin echo sequence during which only the coupling evolves. After the spin echo the product operators are:

+ I1y cos(πJISτ1)
- 2I1xS2z sin(πJISτ1)
+ S2z

A 90°y-pulse on the I-spins and a 90°x-pulse on the S-spins transfer the antiphase magnetisation from I to S via a state of longitudinal two-spin order. The S-spin magnetisation is enhanced by the INEPT transfer.

+ I1y cos(πJISτ1)
- 2I1zS2y sin(πJISτ1)
+ S2y

During the evolution time t1 a second spin echo sandwich is applied, this time only on the I-spins to refocus the coupling.

- I1y cos(πJISτ1) cos(Ω1t1)
+ I1x cos(πJISτ1) sin(Ω1t1)
+ 2I1zS2y sin(πJISτ1) cos(Ω2t1)
- 2I1zS2x sin(πJISτ1) sin(Ω2t1)
+ S2y cos(Ω2t1)
- S2x sin(Ω2t1)

A 90°x-pulse on both I- and S-spins converts the antiphase magnetisation back to the I-spins. This results in an antiphase term 2I1yS2z and the double-quantum coherence 2I1yS2x which will remain unobservable.

- I1z cos(πJISτ1) cos(Ω1t1)
+ I1z cos(πJISτ1) sin(Ω1t1)
+ 2I1yS2z sin(πJISτ1) cos(Ω2t1)
- 2I1yS2x sin(πJISτ1) sin(Ω2t1)
+ S2z cos(Ω2t1)
- S2x sin(Ω2t1)

For simpler and more intense signals it is useful to decouple the spins during acquisition of the FID. Since the antiphase signals which carry the correlation information would collapse to zero when decoupled, they must be transformed back to single quantum coherences by another spin echo sandwich. A delay time of τ2 = 1/(4JIS) causes maximal magnetisation on I1y.

- 2I1yS2z sin(πJISτ1) cos(Ω2t1) cos(πJISτ2)
+ I1x sin(πJISτ1) cos(Ω2t1) sin(πJISτ2)

All the signals from uncoupled protons, which overlap with the signals in the HSQC spectrum, must be suppressed using phase cycling.
