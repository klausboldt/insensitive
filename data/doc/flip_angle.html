Flip angle

The flip angle β is the angle around which a spin vector is flipped when irradiated with an rf-pulse. The angle can be determined by tuning the power and duration of the pulse.

In routine experiments a flip angle is selected so that the optimum between T1 relaxation times and the recording time is achieved for the best signal-to-noise ratio. A large angle of 90° will yield high signal intensity but needs long relaxation times. A smaller angle allows for higher repetition rates on the cost of sensitivity. The optimum value for β is called the Ernst angle.

A typical value is 30° for the 1D-experiment (Bruker code: zg30).
