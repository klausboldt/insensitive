Populations

An operator for a quantum mechanical system, like the Hamiltonian, has a number of eigenstates which are time-independent. These correspond to the spins being oriented either in parallel (α) or antiparallel (β) to the external magnetic field. The α-state translates into a magnetic moment along the z-axis (red vector), the β-state is a magnetic moment along -z.

Each spin can be in any of these states or in a superposition of any number of states and thus populate a number of eigenstates. The real coefficients for these states are called populations. In a density matrix the populations are represented by the diagonal elements.

The simplest model of NMR represents only the energy levels and populations, as well as the transitions between energy levels. In case of system of two coupled spins 1/2 there are four possible energy levels.

This model explains the NMR signal as transitions between states. Only the single quantum transitions with the transition probability W1 are observable. Other transitions that involve the flip of two or more spins can be induced by dipole-dipole interactions and are important for relaxation.

In a three spin system the energy levels are drawn in a pseudo-3D perspective. Each colour (red, blue, orange) stands for one kind of spins being flipped with the violet arrows denoting multi-quantum transitions.
