z-Filter

A z-filter is a pulse sequence block used to eliminate phase and multiplet distortions in two-dimensional spectra. This is done by inserting two 90° pulses spaced by a variable delay time τ, which is varied stochastically between experiments. All undesired coherences, which are not transformed into z-magnetisation, will be averaged out when combining all experiments with different τ, while the stored longitudinal magnetisation is recovered by the second pulse.

An improved version of the z-filter uses a gradient pulse to dephase the transverse magnetisation and no variation of τ is needed.

z-COSY

The block is used in the z-filtered COSY experiment, a variant of the COSY spectrum that yields simple spectra, especially for strongly coupled systems, with clean diagonal peaks and amplitudes that at proportional to the lines in a 1D spectrum. The experiment shows the same signals as the E.COSY, albeit with incomplete suppression of the unwanted signals.

In a compromise between sensitivity and the suppression of NOESY-like signals short angle pulses of 20° are used for the z-filter.
