Rotating frame

In the laboratory frame the spins precess around the axis of the applied field with the Larmor frequency ω0 that is defined by the field strength B and the gyromagnetic ratio γ of the nucleus. ω0 is in the order of several hundred MHz, but each spin's Larmor frequency deviates slightly from this frequency due to local field effects from chemical shifts and coupling to other spins. These small shifts are on a much smaller scale than ω0. Therefore the frequency of precession is often given relative to a reference frequency ωref.

This can be visualised as a frame of reference that rotates around the z-axis with the same velocity as the spins do. A spin with a Larmor frequency equal to ωref appears stationary while other spins precess slightly slower or faster on the scale of several Hertz. All vectors in Insensitive are given in the coordinates of the rotating frame.
