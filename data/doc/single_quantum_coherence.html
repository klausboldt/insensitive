Single quantum coherence

A single quantum coherence is a state of one or more coherent spins that corresponds to a change of quantum states of only ±1.

Simple single quantum coherences consist of only one single coherence and an arbitrary number of populations. The I2x operator in a two-spin system for example is made up of four simple single quantum coherences ρα+, ρα-, ρβ+ and ρβ-.

Only simple single quantum coherences are observable. They make up the transverse and antiphase magnetisation. The matrix elements for simple single quantum coherences in the density matrix can be colour coded in Insensitive. Positive coherences are marked in red while negative coherences are marked in green.

In a system of more than two spins single quantum coherences can be constructed of more coherences if all but one cancel each other out. One example is the matrix element ρ+-+. These non-simple quantum coherences are generally not observable.
