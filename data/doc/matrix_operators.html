Matrix operators

The density matrix can be constructed as a linear combination of orthogonal n x n-matrices representing pure spin states. These matrices are called product operators and can be constructed from four basic spin matrices. They are the identity matrix

the z-component which represents the excess population of α-spins in the matrix diagonal

the x-component which represents coherences in x-direction as real, off-diagonal elements

and the y-component which represents coherences in y-direction as imaginary, off-diagonal elements.

For systems of more than one spin the product operators are formed by the direct product of single-spin matrices. x-Magnetisation of spin 1 is the product of Ix with E/2, while for x-magnetisation of spin 2 the direct product of E/2 with Ix must be calculated.

Antiphase magnetisation is the product of z-magnetisation of one spin with x- or y-magnetisation of the second spin.

The operator for the longitudinal two-spin order is the product of two population matrices and represents two spins of which the energy levels are not populated independently.

Multi-quantum coherences are direct products of more than one single quantum coherence.

Propagators
