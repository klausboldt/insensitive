J-Resolved Spectroscopy

The J-resolved 2D spectra differ from most common 2D methods in that they do not correlate different spins by coherence transfer. Instead, resolved spectra display different variables along the two axes, in this case chemical shift and J-coupling.

The pulse sequence consists of a 90° pulse creating obeservable coherences, followed by a spin echo sandwich with a 180° pulse in the centre of the variable evolution time t1 and the spectrum acquisition time t2.

The 180° pulse refocusses the chemical shift, so that the apparent evolution after t1 for each coherence amounts to half the coupling constant. Peaks in the F1 domain appear at precession frequencies of ±1/2 J, while the spectrum spreads normally in the F2 domain.

The spectrum can be skewed to align the peaks of one multiplet at the chemical shift frequency in F2. The splitting due to J-coupling is then solely displayed in the indirect domain and the two variables are separated for easy interpretation.
