Phase factor

In a superposition of states the coefficents cn in the linear combination c1Ψ1 + c2Ψ2 + ... are generally complex numbers. They have therefore an amplitude and a phase. The amplitude is a measure for the weighting of each wave function Ψn in the linear combination, while the phase gives the orientation of the spin vector in the x,y-plane.

A phase shift of the rf-pulse determines the axis around which the spin vectors are rotated during the pulse. A pulse from x with a phase of 0° will cause a rotation around the x-axis. A phase shift of 90° will cause the spin vectors to rotate around the y-axis.

If the phase of a spin is not zero at the beginning of the FID, the signals will show a phase shift. This appears as a deviation of the pure absorption Lozentzian line shape of the real spectrum and a pure dispersion line shape of the imaginary spectrum to a mixture of absorption and dispersion line shapes. This can be corrected by multiplying the spectra with a phase factor. In zero order phase correction this is a constant complex number φ0 that shifts the phase of the whole spectrum, while in first order phase correction the factor φ1 depends linearly on the Larmor frequency and shifts the phase of the signals relatively to each other.
