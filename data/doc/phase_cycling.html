Phase cycling

Phase cycling is a method to suppress signals in the NMR spectrum and selecting certain coherence pathways by systematically varying the phase of pulses in a pulse sequence and of the receiver coil. By adding two spectra that have been recorded with a phase shift of 180° between same spins the resulting spectral lines cancel each other out. This can be used in more complex pulse sequences to remove unwanted peaks, while other peaks of interest are retained. A good example of this technique is the INADEQUATE experiment in which the uncoupled 13C signals are removed to reveal the 13C-13C satellites as antiphase peaks.

If a pulse's phase is changed in a phase cycle this is usually symbolised by denoting the phase as φ instead of x, y, etc.
