PENDANT

The PENDANT experiment (Polarisation ENhancement During Attached Nucleus Testing) is comparable to the DEPT experiment, but shows all possible carbon atoms in a molecule, including those not bound to any hydrogen, with phases depending on the number of coupled hydrogen atoms. Primary and tertiary carbon atoms cause signals pointing upwards, while signals of secondary and quarternary carbon atoms point downwards.

The PENDANT is very similar to the refocussed INEPT sequence, but starts with a pulse on the carbon atoms (S spins) as well as on the protons (I spins). This causes magnetisation of the quarternary carbon atoms to be visible, albeit with less intensity than for other configurations. The intensity values are therefore not comparable.
