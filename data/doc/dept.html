DEPT

The Distortionless Enhancement by Polarisation Transfer experiment is used to differentiate between carbon atoms with different numbers of attached protons. It is similar to the INEPT experiment in that it is enhances the signal strength by transfer of magnetisation from the protons to the carbon atoms. Due to the magnetisation transfer it is more sensitive than the APT experiment, which otherwise shows the same information.

The pulse sequence starts with a 90°x-pulse on the I-spins, creating transversal magnetisation, which is allowed to evolve for a delay τ/2 = 1/(2J).

- I1y cos(Ω1τ/2) cos(πJISτ/2)
+ 2I1xS2z cos(Ω1τ/2) sin(πJISτ/2)
+ I1x sin(Ω1τ/2) cos(πJISτ/2)
+ 2I1yS2z sin(Ω1τ/2) sin(πJISτ/2)

A 180°x-pulse inverts the I-spins' y- and z-components, while a simultaneous 90°x-pulse on the S-spins creates multi-quantum coherences.

+ I1y cos(Ω1τ/2) cos(πJISτ/2)
- 2I1xS2y cos(Ω1τ/2) sin(πJISτ/2)
+ I1x sin(Ω1τ/2) cos(πJISτ/2)
+ 2I1yS2y sin(Ω1τ/2) sin(πJISτ/2)

After another delay time of τ/2 the proton chemical shifts are refocused. The S-spins are then inverted with a 180°x-pulse while the I-spins are rotated around a variable angle θ which reverts the multi-quantum coherences back to antiphase magnetisation. A third delay of τ/2 lets the antiphase terms decay to pure, transversal magnetisation to allow for decoupling.

The intensity of the 13C signals depends on the flip angle θ. These are different for different configurations of the carbon atom (CH blue, CH2 violet, CH3 red):

An angle of θ = 135° causes CH and CH3 signals to have positive absorption lines while CH2 have negative absorption lines. A 90° angle only shows CH groups, while a 45° pulse causes all protonated carbon atoms to appear as positive absorption lines. All three variations need to be combined to yield unambiguous data. Quaternary carbon atoms and signals from deuterated solvents are removed by phase cycling.

The similar PENDANT experiment (Polarisation ENhancement During Attached Nucleus Testing) also shows quaternary carbon atoms.
