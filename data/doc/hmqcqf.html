HMQC

The Heteronuclear Multi-Quantum Correlation experiment is a two-dimensional method to record single-bond correlation spectra between protons and hetero spins. It is very similar to the HSQC experiment, but is better suited for small molecules due to different relaxation behaviour. Additionally and in contrary to the HSQC, it displays JH,H-coupling information. For the HMQC spectrum the magnetisation of the protons is being detected, which results in higher signal strength compared to heteronuclear COSY.

Set up a two-spin system with one I-spin with a chemical shift of Ω1 = -1.8 Hz and an S-spin with a chemical shift of Ω2 = 2.5 Hz. Set the coupling constant to J = 0.25 Hz.

The pulse sequence starts with a 90°x-pulse on the I-spins to create transverse magnetisation. This is allowed to evolve under chemical shift and scalar coupling operators for the time τ = 1/(2JIS), resulting in the product operators:

- I1y cos(Ω1τ) cos(πJISτ)
+ 2I1xS2z cos(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ)
+ I1x sin(Ω1τ) cos(πJISτ)
+ 2I1yS2z sin(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ)

A 90°x-pulse on the S-spins converts the antiphase terms to multi-quantum coherences:

- I1y cos(Ω1τ) cos(πJISτ)
- 2I1xS2y cos(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ)
+ I1x sin(Ω1τ) cos(πJISτ)
- 2I1yS2y sin(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ)

During the evolution time t1 a 180°x-pulse is applied to the I-spins. This spin echo sandwich causes only the chemical shifts of spin S to evolve.

+ 2I1xS2y cos(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ) cos(Ω2t1)
+ 2I1yS2y sin(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ) sin(Ω2t1)

Another 90°x-pulse on the S-spins converts the multi-quantum coherences back to antiphase terms:

+ 2I1xS2z cos(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ) cos(Ω2t1)
+ 2I1yS2z sin(Ω1τ) sin(πJISτ) sin(Ω2t1)

The magnetisation is allowed to evolve back into single-quantum coherences during another delay time τ and is then recorded under broadband decoupling of the S-spins.
